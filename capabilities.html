Computing Research Group Capabilities

Capabilities

There are four micro teams within the Voice Computing Research Group, namely, ASR, TTS, CNLP, HCI & Accessibility.

ASR (Automatic Speech Recognition) Team members: Dr Jaco Badenhorst (team lead), Dr Rynhardt Kruger, Mr Franco Mak and Mr Privolin Naidoo. Automatic speech recognition (ASR) takes speech as input and converts it to text (which can be processed/analysed/mined in various ways). The ASR team aims to develop ASR systems for South Africa’s official languages.

For well-resourced languages, the advent of deep architecture modelling resulted in significant error rate reductions. Since ASR systems are sensitive to the received speech input data, past training of robust ASR models has always required careful consideration of variations in recorded data. Signal variations such as different speaker accents and other conditions that affect the signal-to-noise ratio are important factors for model estimation.



One of the advantages of deep architecture techniques is that these models now successfully incorporate speech information from larger, more diverse datasets than what was previously possible. In practice, it allows systems to effectively learn from more training examples containing different speaking styles and accents.

TTS (Text-to-Speech)

Team members: Mr Aby Louw (team lead).

Text-to-speech systems (TTS) take text as input and generate synthetic speech as output.

The TTS team develops synthetic voices for South Africa’s written official language. The team has developed Qfrency, a commercial product that provides synthetic voices in eleven of the official languages of South Africa.

CNLP (Controlled Natural Language Processing)

Team members: Dr Laurette Marais (team lead) and Mr Sthembiso Mkhwanazi.

The CNLP team’s aim is to develop and apply computational grammars for the South African languages in research and development projects.

Our current focus is on education, specifically literacy and language learning. Our target audience currently comprises teachers and learners, but in principle, anyone interested in domain-specific, high-quality natural language understanding and generation might benefit from our work. A central focus of the team is the development of resource grammars for the South African languages.

These grammars enable us to rapidly develop application grammars for specific domains but have also shown promise in supporting machine learning natural language processing (NLP) for the South African languages via data annotation and augmentation.

HCI (Human-Computer Interaction) & Accessibility

Team members: Ms Ilana Wilken (TL), Ms Mamolapi Mothomoholo and Mr Willem van der Walt.

The HCI team’s main aim is to investigate and integrate the human element of human language technology (HLT) research. We do this by assisting the TTS, ASR and CNLP teams to develop technologies that are usable and acceptable to the end-users.

For projects with minimum viable products (MVPs) as the end goal, the HCI team is involved throughout the project. We draft requirement specifications, we develop wireframes, we create style guides to inform the look and feel of the products, we conduct internal testing and lastly, we conduct end-user testing through piloting. These steps are typically iterative, and the outputs are refined throughout the project’s lifecycle.

The HCI team is also involved in other projects in the field of digital humanities. These projects involve audits of HLTs or investigations into how South Africans perceive and use HLTs. These types of projects inform much of the work the other micro teams conduct since the outcomes of such projects provide valuable information for their team strategies.
