About us

South Africa has a diverse population with 12 official languages, differing levels of literacy, and a mixed economy. Language forms a crucial part of information exchange, and finding suitable modes and methods of improving language-based information exchange in such a diverse society is of paramount importance. In South Africa, language barriers occur alongside and exacerbate literacy and socio-economic barriers. In order to produce a truly equitable society, where information exchange benefits all its members, language barriers must be addressed. Such barriers may occur in the information exchange between members of society as they interact directly, or they may occur in ways that prevent segments of society from accessing widely available information, but out of reach due to the language in which it is available. Language technology, and spoken language technology in particular, has the potential to address both kinds of language barriers. The Voice Computing Research Group (VCRG) is one of three research groups within the Networked Systems and Applications impact area, situated in the Next Generation Enterprises and Institutions cluster at the CSIR. The VCRG has been researching and developing capabilities and technologies in the voice computing field over the past 20 years and focuses on localising human language technologies for South Africaâ€™s written official languages. The VCRG consists of 12 team members and is currently being led by Dr Avashna Govender, with Mrs Kogie Pillay as Project Manager. The team is made up of engineers, computer scientists and social scientists with a vast array of skills and capabilities.
